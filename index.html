Start With Gratitude

Loyalty / Rewards

Reservation

Guests Waiting (Host + Server Both Own This)

We appreciate your patience so much. You chose to spend your morning with us and that matters.

Both the host and the waiting server acknowledge the wait. Gratitude is not one person's job.

Find Reasons to Compliment

A genuine compliment makes a guest feel seen. Not flattered. Seen. Look for something real and name it.

Example
That's a beautiful sweater, Mrs. Higgins.
Example
I love that bag. Great choice.
Example
Your kids are so well-behaved. What a great little crew.
Example
Happy birthday, by the way. We're so glad you're spending it with us.
  • Something they're wearing, something they said, something about their group
  • Use their name if you have it. A name is the best compliment you can pay.
  • One genuine compliment lands better than five generic ones
  • Never force it. If it isn't real, don't say it.

Gratitude is not a script. It's a posture. Every touchpoint is a chance to say: we know you had a choice, and we're glad you chose us.

The Morning Belle Standard
👁
Observational Hospitality
See everything. Say nothing twice.
02
Read the Table
  • Guest scanning the room is looking for something. Get there first.
  • Empty glass is not a question. It's a cue. Refill it or ask.
  • Plate pushed to the edge means they're done. Clear it.
  • Two people talking quietly and not looking around want space. Give it.
  • Guest who looks tense or bothered didn't start that way. Find out what happened and fix it.
  • Eye contact from a seated guest means they need you. Respond immediately.
  • Guests still wearing their coats? The room might be cold. Check the temperature before they have to ask.
Read the Room

Before the first guest walks in, the room has to be ready.

  • Floors clean? Every corner, not just the aisle.
  • Bathrooms stocked, clean, and smelling right? Check on a schedule, not when someone complains.
  • Tables fully set? Salt, pepper, syrup, hot sauce, ketchup, sugar caddy.
  • Menus clean? A sticky menu is a first impression.
  • Music right for the daypart? Energy in the room starts with the room.
Anticipate the Need
  • Shareable order? Bring extra plates before they ask.
  • Multiple dishes incoming? Lay out silverware for everyone before food arrives.
  • Table with a baby or toddler? Offer the high chair before the parent wrestles with it.
  • Guest poured their own water from the carafe? You should have been there first.
  • Toast, bread, or a pastry ordered? Butter and jam on the table, already.
  • Hot sauce or ketchup ordered last time? Have it there when they sit down.
Cleanliness Is Hospitality
  • A clean floor says we care before the food arrives.
  • A dirty bathroom says we stopped caring after you walked in.
  • Every Gardener owns the room. Not just their section.
  • If you see it, fix it. That is the standard.
The Language of Observation
Never Say
Is everything okay?
Say This Instead
What can I bring you right now?
Say This
I noticed your glass was getting low.
Say This
I wanted to bring extra plates in case you wanted to share.
Say This
What can I grab to make you more comfortable?

The guest feels seen before they ask for anything. That's the whole standard.

Uncommon Hospitality

Beverage First
Lead with the drink. The check follows.
03
The Standard Greeting
Never Say
What can I getcha?
Always Say
Would you like to try a Morning Belle mimosa or brunch cocktail? If you're not looking for a cocktail, we have our Sunrise Squeeze and iced lattes.
Table Reads + Specific Suggestions
  • Date table of two — Belle Mimosa, Morning Spritz
  • Group table — Bottle of champagne, "squeeze in more flavor"
  • Sober or light drinker — Sunrise Squeeze, Bloom and Bubbles Zero
  • Coffee moment — Spiked Cold Brew, Wake Up Call
  • Guest mentions beer — Founders Solid Gold Lager, Beermosa
Refill Is a Revenue Moment

A second round of mimosas is not a favor. It's an opportunity. Always ask before the glass is empty.

Say This
Would you like another mimosa while you enjoy your meal?

Name the drink. Describe it. Make them want it. "We have drinks" sells nothing.

☕
Lobby Comfort
The wait is a sales opportunity.
05
The Default Sample

Chit Chats. Warm, easy, low cost, high memorability. Every waiting guest is a candidate.

Say This
While you wait, would you like to try our Chit Chats? Warm cinnamon doughnut bites with Morning Belle sprinkles. On us.
Or This
Here's a little something from us while we get your table ready.
Why It Works
  • Removes friction from the wait experience
  • Builds an emotional memory before the guest sits down
  • Guests who receive something during the wait tip more and return more often
  • It is the $30 experience starting in the lobby

Lobby comfort is not a cost. It's the cheapest marketing we do.

Catering
Every table is a catering lead.
06
Trigger Moments
  • Guest mentions an upcoming event, birthday, office, or group gathering
  • Large table with coworkers
  • Guest says "I love this place" or "we come here all the time"
  • Guest is picking up to-go and mentions a team or group
The Pitch
Say This
Did you know we cater? We can bring Morning Belle to your office or your next event. Cinnamon Roll French Toast for the whole team. Our cards are at the host stand.
Campaign Language
Bring the Belle. Everyday luxury. Delivered. Order online or call it in.

Catering is off-site, transactional, and convenience-driven. It is Morning Belle, delivered. Keep the message simple.

The four walls are the best catering sales channel we have. Every server is a catering rep.
